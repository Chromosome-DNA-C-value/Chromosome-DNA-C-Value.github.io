20. Cytogenetic mechanism (s) underlying evolution:The evolution of genetic complexity in,

and divergence between, taxa of the genus Arachis in general and taxa in section Arachis in

particular was accompanied by large scale variation in chromosome number, 2C nuclear

DNA amounts and chromosomal repatterning. Variability in the number, size and location of

18S - 5.8S - 26S and 5S ribosomal gene families, and that of C - bands, and centromeric and

(or) interstitial DAPI+bands were other features in the chromosomal evolution of Arachis

taxa. Polyploidy is not particularly common in genus Arachis, although in one case it has

been important in the origin and evolution of a distinct allotetraploid species, A.hypogaea, in

association with characteristics of tremendous economic value.
