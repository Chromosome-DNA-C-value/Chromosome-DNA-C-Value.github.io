India, till mid 1970s, was in the field of chromosome research, among the first ten countries in the world. India has a long tradition of plant cytogenetics and enormous amount of data has been accumulated in the last approximately 75 years. Major work has emanated from various centers all across the country by some internationally recognized  groups led by the personalities well known in this field. In addition to these, many other  universities and Institutes have been engaged in this work on diverse plant groups and  especially economically important plant species. This has been possible not only because   traditional cytological work required less resources but also because of the highly trained  persons who developed chromosome squashing as an art. This has resulted in publications  which not only deal with chromosome numbers but also detailed biosystematic studies on   a number of economic plants. A moderate estimate of these publications may run into few  thousand. Moreover, this enormous data is dispersed in many different journals which are  required to be organized in to a flexible and widely accessible tool.  The researchers/teachers in any branch /discipline of Plant Sciences need to have prior  knowledge with respect to chromosomes (sole heredity component, passed from one  generation to another), for example, the basic number and zygotic numbers, nature and  levels of ploidy, random/non-random recombination, gene rich and gene poor regions of  chromosomes, levels of hybridity, and so on, failing which the results obtained and (or)  conclusions drawn may be highly questionable and not repeatable. The ongoing database,  therefore, will be of importance from both basic and practical standpoint for one to all  plant scientists. Further, it is explicitly clear in the minds of the present investigators that  the Comprehensive Chromosome Database of the kind and magnitude we undertook is  not available anywhere in the world. Our Database in stricter terms shows Chromosome  Numbers along with comprehensive cytogenetic account / data along with genome size  data for direct relevance / understanding / information to all plant scientists in contrast to  the current databases available Worldwide with chromosome numbers only. The database  being developed will be a reference book in all the libraries, not

 

only in India but other  countries as well to investigators in different disciplines.
